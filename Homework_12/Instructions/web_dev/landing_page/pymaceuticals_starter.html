Pymaceuticals Inc.¶

        The following is an analysis of three treatments (in respect to our control, a placebo) and their effects on squamous cell carcinoma (SCC), a common form of skin cancer and the treatments are as follows:

1. Capomulin, 
2. Ketapril, and
3. Infubinol.

        The most significant of the trends from our analysis shows that Capomulin was the only drug to decrease the tumor volume over the 45-day trial, slow down the spread of metastatic sites, and prolong the largest number of lifespans of the mice. Intriguingly, in the respect of reducing tumor volume, Ketapril performed worse than the placebo, but fared better in terms of survival rate. Lastly, Infubinol performed worse than the placebo in prolonging the lifespans of the mice, yet fared better than both Ketapril and the placebo in terms of reducing tumor volume.

*Data visualizations are listed below.
